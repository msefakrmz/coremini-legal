TimeOff — Privacy Policy

Version 1.0 · Effective date: 22 June 2026
Provider / Data Controller: Coremini · Contact: info@coremini.com.tr

Summary. TimeOff silences calls from the work contacts you choose while you are off the clock. It is engineered to be privacy-by-design: there is no user account, no sign-in, and no Coremini server that receives your data. Your contacts, location and call information are processed solely on your device and are never transmitted to us. A small number of features (address search, map display, optional advertising, and purchases) involve third-party services; these are described in Sections 6–7. This summary is for convenience only; the full policy below governs.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how personal data is handled in connection with the TimeOff mobile application (the "App") for Android and iOS, published by Coremini ("Coremini", "we", "us"). It also serves, for users in the Republic of Türkiye, as the controller's disclosure ("aydınlatma metni") under Article 10 of the Law on the Protection of Personal Data No. 6698 ("KVKK"), and, for users in the European Economic Area and the United Kingdom, as the information notice under Articles 13–14 of the General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR"). It does not apply to third-party services that you access through the App, which are governed by their own policies (see Section 6).

2. Data controller and contact

For the limited processing for which a controller is identifiable (see Section 3), the controller is Coremini. You can contact us, including to exercise your rights, at info@coremini.com.tr. We have not appointed a statutory Data Protection Officer because we are not required to; data-protection enquiries are handled at the contact address above.

3. Our processing model — privacy by design

TimeOff's core function — deciding whether to silence an incoming call based on the work contacts you selected, your workplace location, and your working hours or shift — is performed entirely on your device. Coremini operates no backend that collects, receives, stores, or has access to your contacts, your location, or your call information. Because we never obtain that data, Coremini does not act as a data controller in respect of that on-device processing; you remain in control of it. We act as a controller only for the narrow categories of data that necessarily leave your device when you use a feature that relies on a third party (Section 6), and even then we minimise such data.

6. Recipients and third-party services

We do not sell your personal data and we do not share it with third parties for their own marketing. Personal data leaves your device only in the limited cases below, each as an independent service you invoke:

a) OpenStreetMap Foundation — address search & map tiles

When you search for a workplace address, the text you type and your app-language are sent to the OpenStreetMap Nominatim service to return coordinates; displaying the map loads tiles from OpenStreetMap servers, which receive standard request metadata (e.g. the map area requested and your IP address). See the OpenStreetMap Foundation Privacy Policy.

b) Google — advertising (Google AdMob) and purchases (Google Play Billing)

If advertising is enabled in the free tier (Android only), Google AdMob (provided in the EEA/UK by Google Ireland Limited) may process advertising identifiers and usage signals to serve and measure ads; where required, consent is collected through Google's User Messaging Platform before personalised ads are shown, and buying Premium removes ads. Purchases are processed by Google Play Billing; Google handles the payment and we receive only your entitlement status — we never receive your payment-card details. See Google's Privacy Policy and how Google uses data.

These providers act as independent controllers and/or our processors for the specific data described, under their own terms.

7. International data transfers

Because on-device data never leaves your device, it is not transferred internationally by us. For the third-party services in Section 6, data may be processed on servers outside your country, including in the United States. Those providers rely on their own transfer mechanisms — for transfers from the EEA/UK these include the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses and, where applicable, adequacy decisions (e.g. the EU–US Data Privacy Framework). For transfers from Türkiye, such processing is undertaken under the conditions of KVKK Article 9, including, where applicable, your explicit consent.

8. Data retention

On-device data is retained on your device until you remove it. You can clear the silenced-call history at any time from the "Who called me" screen, edit or delete your work contacts and workplaces in the App, and uninstalling the App permanently deletes all of its locally stored data. We hold no server-side copy to retain. Third-party providers (Section 6) retain data under their own policies.

9. Security measures

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures (GDPR Art. 32; KVKK Art. 12). In particular, sensitive on-device data (silenced-call numbers, your work-contacts list, and workplace coordinates) is encrypted at rest using the operating system's hardware-backed keystore (AES-256), and automatic cloud backup and device-to-device transfer of that data are disabled so it cannot leave your device through a backup. Network requests to the third-party services above use encryption in transit (HTTPS). No method of storage or transmission is completely secure; you also help protect your data by securing your device.

We respond without undue delay and in any event within the statutory periods (generally within one month under the GDPR; within 30 days under the KVKK). Exercising these rights is free unless a request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

11. Automated decision-making and profiling

The App automatically decides whether to silence a given call, based solely on the rules you configured (your chosen contacts, your workplace, and your hours/shift) and executed on your device. This does not produce legal or similarly significant effects on you within the meaning of GDPR Article 22, and it involves no profiling by Coremini. You can change or disable the rules, or turn protection off, at any time.

12. Children's privacy

TimeOff is intended for a general adult audience (working users) and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly process the personal data of children under the age of 16 (or the lower age provided by applicable national law, where relevant). If you believe a child has provided personal data, contact us and we will assist.

13. Local storage and SDKs

The App stores its settings and the data described above in the device's local app storage (encrypted where sensitive). It does not use web cookies. It includes the software development kits necessary for the features described — map rendering, geocoding, geofencing, local notifications, in-app purchasing, and (free tier) advertising — each operating as described in this policy.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the App or the law evolves. Material changes will be indicated by updating the version and effective date above and, where appropriate, by an in-app notice. Continued use after an update constitutes acknowledgement of the revised policy to the extent permitted by law.
